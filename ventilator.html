About the Ventilator

Hopefully we are at an inflection point in the COVID-19 pandemic because of the readily available vaccines and distribution, but there is still a need for a reliable, low cost, accurate, and transportable ventilator. Because of recent events, ventilators have shown their effectiveness in treating respiratory distress illnesses accelerating the frequency that this treatment is used and the market size. The total ventilator market in 2027 is estimated to reach 807 Million dollars with ventilators ranging from bag valve masks to thirty thousand dollar clinical ventilators.

There are numerous complications associated with the COVID-19 virus, but Acute Respiratory Distress Syndrome, or ARDS, is the paramount ailment among the most severe patients. Symptoms of ARDS include shortness of breath, increased respiratory rate, and decreased blood oxygenation levels. Our design will meet all requirements set forth by the FDA and AAMI for emergency use ventilators.

The conventional ventilators in today's market have various mechanisms in the air delivery system. Our design is mainly based on an airbellow to realize the compression. Compared with the BVM and clinical ventilator, our design not only has the advantage in lower cost, but also gets better functionality, and most importantly, it is portable, easy to pack up and well-controlled by remoting.
To operate the ventilator, three inputs will be required: the respiration rate, tidal volume, and the IE ratio of the patient. Then, a constant volume of air will be delivered into the patient's lung. There is also an alarm system which monitors the performance. The pressure sensors and the flow meter together will ensure the flow rate, volume, and pressure of the air flow are within acceptable range.

The Low-Cost COVID-19 Ventilator can be manufactured for under $650 and contains a disposable patient assembly for less than $100 and reusable assembly. Furthermore, the convenient design of the compression system increases the mobility of the unit. In closing, for just a small initial investment, our Low Cost Ventilator for COVID-19 Patients can, and will, meet the demands of any future pandemics.
The G3_ME Low Cost COVID-19 Ventilator was shown to be feasible for mandatory volume-controlled non-invasive mechanical ventilation. Air with constant volume delivered into a patient's lung by a compression mechanism and a closed control loop. There is currently only one mode in the ventilator, there is however, room for expansion in the programming and control strategy to include a secondary mode. This ventilator comes with mandatory mode in which air is delivered at a predetermined cycle.
