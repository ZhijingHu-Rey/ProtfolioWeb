Sophomore CAD Model Design Project on Multi-function Drone


Compute-Aided Design
School of Engineering @ Rutgers University - New Brunswick

Group Memebers
Zhijing Hu, Huan Min, Sara Atzbi, Joshua Salmon, Ava Zawacki

About our project

The goal of our project is to design a Multi-function machine drone with animation using SolidWorks. Since it is only a project for sophomore students, the designing work is kind of simple without considering converting all the parts in the real world.

In this project, we designed our own machine drone with multi-function. The mechanism mates between the assemblies are gears and racks, they are used between fans and motors, and in robot arm, respectively.

The main body of the machine drone contains 1 main box, 2 side connections, 1 middle connection, 1 bottom connection, and 2 wings connections. In the landing set, there are 4 landing rods, 4 landing feet, 2 small connections and 3 landing frames. Totally 6 drone fans with wings are designed, and connected with 6 motor respectively. We used DC motor in our design, which contains 2 outer shields, 1 middle coil with magnet and metal wires, 1 small loop connection and 2 side small protections.

Based on the original functions of drone, we added a robot arm and a foldable piston device attached to the bottom of the drone. In the robot arm part, there are 4 pieces of connection and 1 main hand using rack mate to realize the grabbing motion. As for piston, there are 4 moveable connections, 2 fixed rectangular boxes, 2 main rods and 4 small pins. A LED light or camera could be attached to the piston for any needed.
